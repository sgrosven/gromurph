JavaScore - Penalty Panel

This panel lets you enter penalty information for an entry in a race.

I've grouped penalties into the following groups. 

Non-Finish penalties:   A boat can only have 1 of these penalties.  If assigned one, the boat does not rank as a finisher for any penalties in a scoring system that might care how many boats finish.

Disqualification Penalties.  This is a bit of a misnomer, it include boats that have not technically been disqualified, but the effect is analogous.  These are penalties that will override a boats finish position completely.  If the boat is assigned one of these penalties, then boats finishing after them will have the finishes adjusted upwards.   A boat can only be assigned one of these penalties.  

Standard Percentage Penalties: These are the percentage scoring penalties. These are percentage penaltyies are calculated as per RRS 44. It's uncommon but boats can be assigned more that one of these penalties, in combination.   Several do not make sense to assign in combination.  It is the scoring system's job to make sense of it.  Quickly here's a summary of what these penalties mean:

  • 1st, 2nd, 3rd Z-Flag - boat received one or more penalties under RRS 30.2.
  • Check-in failure - boat is assigned a penalty for failing to check-in properly. NOTE: this is beyond the RRS, and the SI's for the regatta must provide for this penalty.  The amount of the penalty is a percentage and is defined under the Advanced Scoring Options panel within the main regatta parameters
  • After Race Penalty - this is an scoring penalty generally affiliated with Arbitration. It is basically a 40% penalty with an adjustable "cap" to be either 40% or half the distance between a 20% penalty and a DNF. it requires SI language to implement. See ussailing.org/judges/arbitration for more details on the after race penalty. See Advanced Scoring Options to change the base 40% to something else.

    • Custom Percentage Penalties: These are two customize percentage penalties. Check the appropriate box for a scoring percentage or a elapsed time percentage. Then use the text field to enter the percentage amount.

    Redress/Manual: These are the scoring modifications that often come out of redress hearings. Also just scoring unusual happenings. You can choose from among several types/amounts of adjustments. And you can customize the label that shows on the score sheets (Label). The options include:

    • None: the default.
    • Avg All Races: boat will receive a score equal to the average of other finishes in accordance with RRS A10(a) (2013 Rules).  NOTE: check with the protest committee when they award this penalty as they may say average points but not know about the A10(a) definition.  If the jury intends something other than A4(a), you'll have to manually enter the points via "Points" option.
    • Points: if a boat gets some oddball points that the scoring program doesn't deal with so the scorer must enter this manually. Select the Points button, then enter the score the boat should receive in the text box. I recommend that you use the Note field to explain to the competitors.
    • Elapsed/Corrected Time. JavaScore now supports two time-based penalties:  one applied to a boat's elapsed time, the other to its corrected time. Use the text field to enter the raw amount of time to be added. (Use a minus sign to reduce a boat's time).

    NOTE: for both Manual and Redress, if the boat ranks as a finisher, it should have a valid finish position, and boats finishing below it will not generally have their positions moved up.   If the boat did not finish, it should have one of the non-finishing penalties assigned to it, in addition to the MAN or RDG penalty.

    Label: Here you can check "RDG" to have a simple RDG label shown on the scoring reports. Or select "Other" and use the text box to enter whatever wacky abbreviation you (or the protest committee) want to be shown on the reports. Use the Notes field below to explain the abbreviation

    Note field: Enter any notes that apply to this penalty here.  They will be printed as footnotes on the scoring reports.  You might use this field to explain an unusual penalty score, or reference a Hearing number for a DSQ or RDG. 

    STANDARD ABBREVIATIONS on the RRS:

    DNC Did not start; did not come to the starting area
    DNS Did not start (other than DNC and OCS)
    OCS Did not start; on the course side of the starting line and broke rule
    29.1 or 30.1
    ZFP 20% penalty under rule 30.2
    BFD Disqualification under rule 30.3
    SCP Took a scoring penalty under rule 44.3
    DNF Did not finish
    RET Retired
    DSQ Disqualification
    DNE Disqualification not excludable under rule 88.3(b)
    DGM Disqualification for gross misconduct RDG Redress given

    DPI Discretionary Penalty Imposed.

    Javascore also supports the following non-standard penalties:

    TME Time penalty on elapsed time
    AVG Average points
    TMC Time penalty on corrected time
    ZFP2, ZFP3 - a 2nd or 3rd occurrence of ZFP on the same race
